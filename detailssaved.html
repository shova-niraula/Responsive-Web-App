Everyday Ease
Mindfully Moving From Burnout to Balance

(Shanti Douglas)

The repercussions of constant stress and pressure are extreme and can easily lead to a state of burnout. In this interactive and reflective workshop you’ll be guided to create an achievable action plan that will get you back on course, saying NO to burnout and excessive stress and YES to the loves of your life. Have more ease and mindful presence become the new norm.

Using Mindfulness to ReGroup and Refocus for Success

(Jazz Jackson)

Navigating life can be quite a hard and challenging experience. By leveraging mindfulness, it will allow for you to regroup and refocus. By doing so, this will increase your ability to be successful while focusing on your purpose.

Learn to Thrive-
Contemplative Practices for Everyday Life

(Eliza Andrews Richmond)

You are what you repeatedly do. How you use your body, your mind, and your energy determines so much of your day to day experience. Learn practical methods for welcoming mindfulness and contemplative practices into your lifestyle, from basic movement and breathing techniques to mindful eating, and more. Discover a sense of presence, clarity, and awareness.

The Power of Moments

(Tracy Dow)

Certain experiences can have extra ordinary impact. Learn how you can create some extraordinary experiences using elevation, insight, pride, and connection. Based on the book "The Power of Moments" by Chip and Dan Heath.

Mindfulness in Sport

(DR. Mark Hecox)

The practice of developing a mindfulness toolkit is done from a variety of settings and for many reasons. Sport offers many opportunities for both the development and practice of mindfulness techniques. While visualization has been used extensively in sport, meditation and the application of mindfulness offers many additional benefits to athletic performance and athlete well-being.

for the Busy Professional (Shanti Douglas)

(Shanti Douglas)

With so much to do, it seems like there’s never enough time or energy left to take care of it all. How could you even think of adding some mindfulness or meditation to your day then? Using the Foundations of Mindfulness
to Leverage Retention in Business Courses and Programs

(Susan T. Sadowski)

This session will address the designated Why Today? Why Tomorrow? inquiry for the conference through a look at some of the foundations of mindfulness and their value for personal reflection and for contemplative pedagogy. The presentation will focus on concepts and applications for mindfulness in business from an experiential learning and from a theoretical and applied view. It will include an overview of a model for the use of mindfulness for ethical decision-making in business contexts as an example of the use of mindfulness practice for support of students’ learning and for leveraging retention across all academic disciplines at SNHU. The presenter is a qualified Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction teacher through the internationally-recognized Professional Teacher Education program at the University of Massachusetts’ School of Medicine Center for Mindfulness founded by Jon Kabat-Zinn in 1979.

Leaders Communicate Mindfully
and at all Levels

(Donna Rustigian Mac)

This workshop supports participants in becoming less distracted and more present to communicating effectively. Because we are living in an unprecedented time of communication overload, important details can fall through the cracks, our people can feel (and often are) unheard. We’re losing the opportunity to fortify relationships while avoiding mistakes and losing valuable employees. Attendees will examine the virtues of becoming more mindful, asking pertinent questions, and listening. We’ll also help participants uncover their greatest communication strengths and vulnerabilities so they can model mindful communication to their workforce.

Overcoming Failure with Mindfulness

(Elizabeth Bonin)

Be present without judgment. It is easier said than done. When we experience failure, we tend to experience an emotional response filled with self-recrimination. This workshop will demonstrate how mindfulness helps us become aware of failure without becoming overwhelmed by it.

Why Run? A Life Story about Health,
Wellbeing, Reflection, and Endurance

(Eklou Romaric Amendah)

The physical influence of running on the human body is clearly documented by scientists, health professionals, scholars and sport professionals (Yeh, Churchill, Brymer & Keith, 2017; Bellocco, Jia, Ye, Lagerros, 2010). It is widely accepted among scholars that running brings energy and strength to muscles. The mental well-being after a run is also well documented (Brown, Mishra, Lee & Bauman, 2000). What is sometimes overlooked or not discussed exhaustively enough are the internal conflicting feelings runners experience during their runs including reflection, emotion, ataraxia and exuberance and excitement experienced while running. Some of these feelings may be opposite but they are amazingly experienced at the same time. The interesting aspect of these feelings is the runner’s awareness of their presence while exercising or running. These feelings have undeniable direct and indirect influences on the runner’s performance during and after each run regardless of the motivation to run (competitive running and recreational running). Running has had a tremendous influence on my personal and professional performances.

Relaxation and Meditation
In the Yoga Tradition

(Rosie Latona)

This workshop will explore the practices of mindfulness, relaxation, and meditation in the yoga tradition. We will review some of the techniques for preparing the mind and body for meditation, as well as methods that can be used to deepen a meditation practice. Whether you are a new or experienced meditator, you will benefit from having time to explore the use of mindfulness, breathing, and relaxation in this workshop and leave feeling calm, clear, and refreshed.

How Mindfulness and Meditation
can Improve Professional and Personal Performance

(Steven Zanella)

While some might feel skeptical regarding the impact of meditation and mindfulness, the number of successful, high-profile people benefiting from these practices continues to grow. So what exactly is it and how can you apply it to your life? In this session, we will explore the real-world, practical benefits of learning to be more “present”, and how this practice can sharpen your focus, reduce stress, improve communication, and create a more positive work/life balance.
